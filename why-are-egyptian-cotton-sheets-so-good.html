Why are Egyptian cotton sheets so good?

August 2024 · 6 minute read
They're prized for their superior softness, durability, and breathability. The distinct feel of Egyptian cotton sheets come from the unique properties of Egyptian cotton itself. Egyptian cotton has extra-long staples, which means each individual fiber is exceptionally long.

Egyptian cotton bed sheets are some of the best you can buy; crisp and noticeably softer than linen or ordinary cotton, and also highly breathable. Grown exclusively in Egypt (naturally), Egyptian cotton is renowned for producing longer, finer yarns when spun compared to standard cotton.

What is the best sheet thread count?

The best sheets typically have a thread count between 200 and 400. Any thread count lower than 180 tends to have a rougher texture. Any number over 400 is most likely an inflated figure due to multi-ply thread, meaning you'll pay a premium price for a sheet that doesn't actually feel any softer.

Why are my sheets pilling?

Pilling is a result of friction; when the fabric is rubbed the fibers can break. For example, pilling can often be seen on fitted sheets near the foot of the bed where frequent abrasive movement (from rough feet) can occur. Even the stubble from shaved body hair, backs and legs can be abrasive enough to cause pilling.

How often should I wash Egyptian Cotton sheets?

Egyptian cotton doesn't pill, and it doesn't irritate dust allergies, but it does require gentle cleaning. Washing your sheets weekly helps keep them clean and soft.

Can I tumble dry Egyptian Cotton sheets?

Egyptian cotton bedding can be tumble dried on a low setting.
