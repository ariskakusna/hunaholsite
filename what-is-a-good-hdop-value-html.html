What is GPS multipath error?

Interference between two radio waves that have travelled various length pathways between the transmitter and the receiver causes multipath error. The receiving of signals not only directly from satellites, but also reflected or diffracted from nearby objects, causes GPS multipath.

What is Global Navigation Satellite System (GNSS) technology?

The acronym GNSS stands for “Global Navigation Satellite System,” and it refers to all global satellite positioning systems. This includes satellite constellations circling the earth’s surface and broadcasting signals that allow users to identify their location.

What is the purpose of a GPS?

The Global Positioning System (GPS) is a constellation of around 30 satellites circling the Earth at a height of 20,000 kilometres. These light-speed signals are intercepted by your GPS receiver, which uses the time it takes for the messages to arrive to compute how far away each satellite is.

What exactly is an RTK survey?

RTK surveying is a relative positioning technique that uses two GNSS antennas to measure locations in real time. RTK surveying necessitates consistent communication between the base and rover units, and it works best with small baselines since the precision of RTK measurements reduces as the baseline length grows.
