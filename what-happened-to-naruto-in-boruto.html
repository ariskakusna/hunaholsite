What Happened To Naruto In Boruto?

August 2024 · 6 minute read

The “Naruto” series, created by Masashi Kishimoto, has been a cornerstone of anime and manga culture for decades. With the conclusion of Naruto’s story in the original series, fans were eager to see what would become of their beloved characters in the sequel, “Boruto: Naruto Next Generations.” This series shifts the focus to Naruto’s son, Boruto Uzumaki, but still retains a significant role for Naruto himself. In this article, we will delve into the events and changes that have shaped Naruto’s life in the world of “Boruto.”

Transition from Hero to Hokage

  • Naruto’s Dream Realized: Naruto Uzumaki, once the mischievous outcast of the Hidden Leaf Village, achieves his lifelong dream of becoming the Seventh Hokage, the leader and protector of his village.
  • Responsibilities and Challenges: As Hokage, Naruto is burdened with administrative duties, decision-making, and maintaining peace, which often keeps him away from his family.

Family Life

  • Marriage to Hinata: Naruto marries Hinata Hyuga, and they have two children, Boruto and Himawari, balancing his duties as a father with his responsibilities as Hokage.
  • Strained Relationship with Boruto: Naruto’s absence due to his role as Hokage leads to a strained relationship with his son, Boruto, who feels neglected and resentful.

Power and Abilities in Adulthood

  • Matured Skills: Naruto retains his immense chakra reserves, mastery of the Rasengan, and Sage Mode abilities, though he uses them less frequently in his role as Hokage.
  • Loss of the Truth-Seeking Balls: After the Fourth Great Ninja War, Naruto no longer has access to the Truth-Seeking Balls, a powerful tool he once wielded.

Threats to the Hidden Leaf

  • New Enemies: The emergence of the Otsutsuki Clan and the organization known as Kara presents new threats that Naruto must confront to protect his village.
  • Protecting the Next Generation: Naruto takes on a mentorship role, guiding the young ninjas of the village, including his son Boruto, to face these growing dangers.

Loss of Kurama

  • The Baryon Mode: In a desperate battle against Isshiki Otsutsuki, Naruto unlocks a new form called Baryon Mode, which exponentially increases his power but has a dire cost.
  • The Ultimate Sacrifice: The use of Baryon Mode results in the death of Kurama, Naruto’s longtime Tailed Beast companion, significantly reducing Naruto’s power level.

Adapting to a New Era

  • Technological Advancements: The world of “Boruto” has seen significant technological progress, and Naruto must adapt to these changes while maintaining the essence of the ninja way.
  • Guiding the Youth: Naruto’s role evolves to focus on guiding the new generation, including his son, to navigate the challenges of this new era.

Relationship with Sasuke

  • Enduring Friendship: Naruto’s bond with Sasuke Uchiha remains strong, with Sasuke serving as his closest ally and advisor in matters concerning the safety of the village.
  • Joint Efforts: Together, they face the threats posed by the Otsutsuki Clan and Kara, showcasing their combined strength and strategic minds.

Confronting Jigen and Isshiki Otsutsuki

  • Initial Defeat: Naruto and Sasuke face off against Jigen, the vessel of Isshiki Otsutsuki, and suffer a crushing defeat, highlighting the formidable power of their new adversaries.
  • Imprisonment and Rescue: Naruto is sealed away by Jigen, only to be later rescued by Boruto and his team, marking a pivotal moment in the father-son relationship.

Supporting Boruto’s Development

  • Mentorship Role: Naruto takes an active role in mentoring Boruto, helping him to harness his unique abilities, including the mysterious Karma seal.
  • Emotional Support: Naruto provides emotional support to Boruto as he grapples with the fear and uncertainty of his Otsutsuki heritage and the Karma seal’s implications.

Continued Leadership

  • Unwavering Resolve: Despite the loss of Kurama and the evolving threats, Naruto remains steadfast in his commitment to protect the village and support its inhabitants.
  • Legacy of Peace: Naruto’s leadership continues to be defined by his desire to maintain the peace he fought so hard to achieve during his youth.

Evolution of Character

  • From Recklessness to Wisdom: Naruto’s character evolves from the impulsive young ninja to a wise and composed leader, reflecting his growth and maturity.
  • Inspiration to All: Naruto’s journey from orphan to Hokage continues to inspire both the characters within the series and the audience who have followed his story.

Challenges as a Father

  • Learning to Connect: Naruto struggles to connect with Boruto, who seeks to forge his own path and escape his father’s shadow.
  • Reconciliation: Through various trials and tribulations, Naruto and Boruto work towards understanding each other, strengthening their bond as father and son.

Role in the Shinobi World

  • Diplomatic Relations: As Hokage, Naruto plays a crucial role in maintaining diplomatic relations with other villages and fostering alliances.
  • Symbol of Peace: Naruto’s reputation as a hero of the Fourth Great Ninja War and as Hokage makes him a symbol of peace and stability in the shinobi world.

Impact on the Series

  • Guiding Light: Naruto’s presence in “Boruto” serves as a guiding light for the new characters, providing a link between the old generation and the new.
  • Continued Relevance: Despite the focus on Boruto and his peers, Naruto remains a central figure in the series, influencing the plot and character development.

FAQ Section

How has Naruto’s role in the village changed in “Boruto”?

Naruto has transitioned from being a hero in the Fourth Great Ninja War to serving as the Seventh Hokage. His role now encompasses administrative duties, decision-making, and protecting the village from new threats.

What is the state of Naruto’s power in “Boruto”?

While Naruto remains one of the most powerful ninjas, the loss of Kurama after using Baryon Mode has significantly reduced his power level. However, he still possesses his other abilities and vast experience.

Does Naruto still have a significant presence in “Boruto”?

Yes, Naruto continues to have a significant presence in “Boruto.” He plays a key role as Hokage, father, and mentor, influencing the story and the development of new characters.

Conclusion

In “Boruto: Naruto Next Generations,” Naruto Uzumaki’s journey evolves from that of a young shinobi with dreams of becoming Hokage to fulfilling those dreams and facing the challenges that come with leadership. His role as a father, mentor, and protector is central to the series, and despite the changes and losses he endures, Naruto’s spirit and dedication to his village remain unwavering. His story continues to inspire, and his impact on the shinobi world is indelible. As the series progresses, fans can expect to see more of how Naruto shapes the future of the Hidden Leaf Village and the world at large.
