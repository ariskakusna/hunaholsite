Every Kendrick Lamar Feature From 2016 So Far

August 2024 · 11 minute read

Kendrick Lamar started the year off strong with the release of untitled. unmastered, but he's also been keeping bust with guest appearances, showing up on range of records from fellow TDE members, underground emcees, and the biggest pop acts going.

No Kendrick feature is the same, but they're all at least worth a listen, most of them much more than that. With that being said, take a look through the many features K-Dot has put out this year in the gallery above. Let us know what your favorites (and least favorites) are in the comments section. Do any of these hint at what the next Kendrick project might sound like?

Freedom

Beyonce - "Freedom" (Feat. Kendrick Lamar)

Black THougHts

Every Kendrick Lamar Feature From 2016 So Far

ScHoolboy Q - "Black THougHts"

"Black THougHts" is an uncredited appearance for Kendrick, but any TDE fan should recognize his distorted voice on the hook from the Blank Face LP deep cut. It's an effective Easter egg that hearkens back to the collective's tendency to work within their small universe of collaborators. It feels like an in-joke between friends, an appeal of TDE that isn't talked about enough.

THat Part

Every Kendrick Lamar Feature From 2016 So Far

ScHoolboy Q - "THat Part (Black Hippy Remix)"

ScHoolboy Q's "THat Part" was the biggest hit to emerge from TDE this year, so it was only fitting that the whole Black Hippy crew hopped on it. Q, Ab-Soul, Jay Rock, and Kendrick always make it difficult to pick a favorite verse, but K-Dot does make a solid bid for the title, entering with an echoing vocal, as he spits dexterous boasts, keeping his rhyme schemes tight and complex throughout. It's hard to beat Kendrick on a purely technical level, and this is him at his most detail-oriented.

Goosebumps

Every Kendrick Lamar Feature From 2016 So Far

Travis Scott - "Goosebumps" (Feat. Kendrick Lamar)

Travis Scott tends to inspire great performances from his collaborators, whether it be Young Thug and Rich Homie on "Mamacita" or Justin Bieber on "Maria, I'm Drunk." His new album, Birds In The Trap Sing McKnight is filled with memorable guest appearances, and Kendrick Lamar might very well have the best of the bunch. It's a reminder is that Kendrick's ability as a performer is on par with his lyricism, as he introduces a delivery that stop-starts, melts, doubles, and squeals. As with most Kendrick verses, the lyrics on paper do not do it justice.

God Is Fair, Sexy Nasty

Every Kendrick Lamar Feature From 2016 So Far

Mac Miller - "God Is Fair, Sexy Nasty" (Feat. Kendrick Lamar)

Mac Miller has suggested in interviews that he wanted to put Kendrick on a song that didn't *sound* like a Kendrick song. "God Is Fair" is definitely an experiment, and putting K-Dot on the chorus is an unconventional move, but given his underrated ability to craft hooks, it's still a smart move, making for a perfect closing track for Mac's The Divine Feminine.

Don't Wanna Know

Every Kendrick Lamar Feature From 2016 So Far

Maroon 5 - "Don't Wanna Know" (Feat. Kendrick Lamar)

The latest Kendrick verse, "Don't Wanna Know" is far from the strongest, suggesting that K-Dot's weakness may be stretching his unconventional style to radio. However, he sounds perfectly comfortable on the track, which seems like a potential hit. More Kendrick on the radio means more kids coming back to To Pimp A Butterfly and good kid, M.A.A.D. city. Hard to argue with that.
